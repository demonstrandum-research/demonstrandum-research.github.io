The statement (use verbatim, adjust tense/length as needed)

These results were produced by Demonstrandum, an autonomous mathematical
research system directed by the author. Demonstrandum orchestrates frontier
AI reasoning models (currently OpenAI's GPT series, Anthropic's Claude, and
Harmonic's Aristotle, among other tools) within a proprietary architecture
for problem selection, sustained attack, adversarial internal review, and
machine verification. Its defining discipline is that no claim rests on any
model's output: every result is reduced to independently checkable
artifacts (kernel-checked Lean proofs, exact certificates verifiable by
standard third-party tools, or dual independently implemented exact
computations) before it is stated. The system's architecture is not
published at this time; its outputs are fully transparent and reproducible
from the accompanying artifacts, and the author takes sole responsibility
for all claims.

Verification

Kernel-checked — the claim is a Lean 4 theorem accepted by the Lean kernel, with its axioms audited and no sorry. Project pages identify the declaration, pinned toolchain, dependencies, and axiom report.

Certificate (dual-checker) — a finite witness is accepted by at least two independently implemented exact checkers, with mutation testing of the checkers.

Every result page carries its own reproduction pointers: the artifact, pinned versions, checker or build command, and the theorem or certificate to inspect.

What verification does not establish

Statement fidelity

Mechanical verification establishes the formal claim presented to the checker. Whether that claim faithfully represents the cited informal statement is reviewed and reported separately.

Novelty

Verification does not establish novelty. Literature searches and their scope are reported separately.

External refereeing

Internal review is not external refereeing. Each result page states its external-review status separately.

Corrections

Corrections are never silent. A corrected claim retains or links its original text, with the date, reason, and corrected record.
