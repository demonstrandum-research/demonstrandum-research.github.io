About

What Demonstrandum does

Demonstrandum is an autonomous mathematical research system directed by independent researcher John Erlbacher. It works on open problems documented in published papers, curated problem databases, and standing records. Every published result includes independently checkable verification artifacts.

The name comes from the Latin demonstrandum, "that which is to be demonstrated" — verification is part of every result.

Methodology

These results were produced by Demonstrandum, an autonomous mathematical research system directed by the author. Demonstrandum orchestrates frontier AI reasoning models (currently OpenAI's GPT series, Anthropic's Claude, and Harmonic's Aristotle, among other tools) within a proprietary architecture for problem selection, sustained attack, adversarial internal review, and machine verification. Its defining discipline is that no claim rests on any model's output: every result is reduced to independently checkable artifacts (kernel-checked Lean proofs, exact certificates verifiable by standard third-party tools, or dual independently implemented exact computations) before it is stated. The system's architecture is not published at this time; its outputs are fully transparent and reproducible from the accompanying artifacts, and the author takes sole responsibility for all claims.

In practice, every result page on this site carries:

  • The exact statement — the theorem as claimed, with exact constants and conventions, and an explicit "what is not claimed" section;
  • A named artifact class — a Lean-kernel proof with a clean axiom audit; a SAT certificate (DRAT/LRAT) checkable by a formally verified third-party checker; an exact interval-arithmetic certificate; or dual independently implemented exact computations;
  • A verification trail — what independently checked the result and how, including negative controls (mutation tests) where applicable;
  • Reproduction commands — how to re-run the check on commodity hardware;
  • A citation block and the artifact download links.

Results are graded, and the grade is stated wherever the result is: a kernel-checked theorem is not the same object as a refereed written proof, and a page never lets the stronger grade rub off on the weaker claim.

AI transparency

The mathematics on this site was produced by an AI system, and we state this plainly on every deliverable. The verification discipline is what makes that a strength: the artifacts are exactly as checkable as anyone else's, and every theorem comes with a proof you can run. John Erlbacher reviews and signs every claim and takes sole responsibility for all of them. Corrections and scrutiny are actively welcome — if anything fails to reproduce, we want to know first.
