Verify it yourself

Nothing on this site asks for trust. Every result is backed by an artifact you can check on your own machine, and every project page states plainly what has been established, by what mechanism, and what has not.

What the grades mean

Kernel-checked â€” the entire claim is a Lean 4 theorem accepted by the Lean kernel, depending on no axioms beyond the three standard mathlib axioms (propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound) and containing no sorry. This is the strongest grade we use.

Certificate (dual-checker) â€” a finite witness (a counterexample, a construction, a computation) accepted by at least two independently written checkers, with mutation testing on the checkers themselves.

Each project page also reports, separately: whether the axioms were audited, whether the formal statement was reviewed against the informal claim it represents (a kernel proof is only as good as the statement it proves), and whether the work has received external review. We never collapse these into one badge, because they are different kinds of confidence.

What verification does not establish

A kernel-checked proof establishes that the formal statement follows from the axioms. It does not by itself establish that the formal statement faithfully captures the informal claim (we review this separately and say so), that the result is novel (we run literature searches and state their scope), or that the work has been externally refereed (we say plainly when it has not). Every project page ends with its limitations.
