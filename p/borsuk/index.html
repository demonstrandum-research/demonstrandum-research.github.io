A Discrete Borsuk Partition Conjecture, Disproved in Lean 4

Overview

Conjecture 3 of Brose, De Loera, López-Campos, and Torres, On Lattice Diameter Segments and A Discrete Borsuk Partition Problem (arXiv:2508.20009 v1, posted 27 August 2025), characterizes the bounded lattice sets whose discrete Borsuk number is 2d.

The conjecture is refuted as stated by a four-point set in dimension 2. The witness and the failure of the printed biconditional are formalized in Lean 4.30.0 with mathlib and kernel-checked against definitions documented line by line from the paper's LaTeX.

The preprint was still v1-only on 11 June 2026, and no published resolution was found. The authors were notified privately.

The frozen statement

The arXiv LaTeX source at paper/sections/Borsuk.tex, line 76, states: “Let S ⊂ Z^d be a bounded set. Then β_Z(S) = 2^d if and only if conv(S) is unimodularly equivalent to a d-cube [0,m]^d for any m ∈ N.”

The result

Counterexample. Let SA = {(0,0), (1,0), (0,1), (3,5)}. Every pairwise difference is primitive, so the lattice diameter is 1 and β_Z(SA) = 4 = 2². On the other hand, |conv(SA) ∩ Z²| = 7. Unimodular equivalence preserves lattice-point counts, while |[0,m]² ∩ Z²| = (m+1)² ≠ 7 for every m. Thus the d = 2 instance, and therefore the all-dimensions biconditional as printed, fails.

Limitations

  • The refutation is of the printed biconditional. The d = 2 instance suffices because the conjecture quantifies over all dimensions.
  • A possible repair remains open here. The witness does not refute the unstated full-set hypothesis S = conv(S) ∩ Z^d. Designed follow-up witnesses B and C were stretch goals and are not claimed.
  • The formal-to-informal bridge is human-checked. The kernel guarantees the Lean statements; their faithfulness to the paper's definitions is documented against the paper's own LaTeX in the reading kit and status report.
  • No external review yet. These results have not been externally refereed. Corrections will be logged, dated, never silent.
