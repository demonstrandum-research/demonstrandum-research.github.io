Sun's Conjecture 4.6(ii) on Sine Permanents, Refuted at p = 29

Overview

Conjecture 4.6 of Zhi-Wei Sun's Arithmetic properties of some permanents (arXiv:2108.07723v7) has two parts: a divisibility assertion for odd composite n, and two sign characterizations for odd primes. The integer-normalized sine and cosecant permanents s_n and s′_p are those of the paper's Theorem 1.6.

Part (ii) is refuted as stated at p = 29, the first prime beyond Sun's table, where both sign clauses fail simultaneously. The values are certified through four algorithmically independent exact layers across five implementations, with independent recomputation audits.

The conjecture was posed in 2021, remained unchanged through v7 in 2022, and that version was still latest on 12 June 2026. Sun's table stops at p = 23, and neither sequence is in OEIS. The author was notified privately.

The frozen statement

The TeX source at paper.tex, line 1109, states: “(i) If n>1 is odd and composite, then s_n ≡ 0 (mod n). (ii) Let p be an odd prime. Then s_p < 0 ⇔ p ≡ 5 (mod 12), and s′_p < 0 ⇔ p ≡ 7 (mod 8).”

The result

At p = 29, s_29 = 1,053,859 > 0 although 29 ≡ 5 (mod 12), while s′_29 = −4,806,838,304 < 0 although 29 ≡ 5 (mod 8) ≠ 7. Thus both clauses of part (ii) fail as stated.

Further failures occur at p = 41 and 53 for the mod-12 clause and at p = 61 for the mod-8 clause. Every computed value satisfies Sun's proved congruences s_p ≡ (−1)^((p+1)/2) and s′_p ≡ 1 (mod p).

Limitations

  • Part (i) is not refuted. It was tested for all 15 odd composite n ≤ 65 and held in every case; it remains open.
  • No replacement sign law is claimed. Values are certified only for primes p ≤ 61 and odd composite n ≤ 65.
