The problem

Estimate gk(N)g_k(N)gk​(N).

Following van Doorn (arXiv:2605.00040), write gkg_kgk​ for the variant in which at most one bib_ibi​ may be non-positive, and hkh_khk​ for the positive variant (all bi≥1b_i \ge 1bi​≥1). Choi, Erdős and Szemerédi proved that h4h_4h4​ is eventually constant — in their words, they were “too lazy to determine” the constant.

In plain language

Take the odd numbers in {1,…,2N}\{1,\ldots,2N\}{1,…,2N}: that is half the set, yet no integers b1,…,b4b_1,\ldots,b_4b1​,…,b4​ have all six pairwise sums inside it — a sum bi+bjb_i + b_jbi​+bj​ is odd only when bib_ibi​ and bjb_jbj​ have opposite parities, and no three integers are pairwise of opposite parity. The question is how much more than half you must take before the structure becomes unavoidable. For four integers with the positivity convention, the answer was known to be some constant eventually; fifty years later, the constant is now a theorem: 4, from n=331,777n = 331{,}777n=331,777 onward — with a machine-checked proof whose every step, from the combinatorial recursion down to the arithmetic, is verified by the Lean kernel. Below the asymptotic range, exhaustive SAT computations settle the value cell by cell, and each computation ships a proof object that an independent, formally verified checker confirms.
