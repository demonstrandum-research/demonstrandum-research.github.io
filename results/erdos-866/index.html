Erdős Problem 866: the eventual value of h₄

Lean 4 + verified SAT certificates John Erlbacher · July 2026

h₄(n) = 4 for all n ≥ 331,777, checked by the Lean 4 kernel — plus the first strict g/h separation beyond k = 3, a factor-32 improvement for g₅, and exact small values with independently checkable SAT certificates.

The paper, Lean development, and complete certificate archive are available on Zenodo. Four values reported below were obtained after the release freeze and are not part of the certified archive; they are marked as such.

The problem

Erdős Problem #866 (Choi–Erdős–Szemerédi, 1975): let k≥3k \ge 3k≥3 and let gk(N)g_k(N)gk​(N) be minimal such that every A⊆{1,…,2N}A \subseteq \{1, \ldots, 2N\}A⊆{1,…,2N} with ∣A∣≥N+gk(N)|A| \ge N + g_k(N)∣A∣≥N+gk​(N) contains all (k2)\binom{k}{2}(2k​) pairwise sums bi+bjb_i + b_jbi​+bj​ of some integers b1,…,bkb_1, \ldots, b_kb1​,…,bk​ (the bib_ibi​ need not lie in AAA). Estimate gk(N)g_k(N)gk​(N).

Following van Doorn (arXiv:2605.00040), write gkg_kgk​ for the variant in which at most one bib_ibi​ may be non-positive, and hkh_khk​ for the positive variant (all bi≥1b_i \ge 1bi​≥1). Choi, Erdős, and Szemerédi proved that h4h_4h4​ is eventually constant but left its value undetermined.

The results

Items marked Lean were verified by the Lean 4 kernel. The development is sorry-free and uses exactly propext, Classical.choice, and Quot.sound. It extends — and byte-pins without modifying — the public formalization accompanying van Doorn’s paper.

Theorem (Lean: Erdos866.h4_eq_4). h4(n)=4h_4(n) = 4h4​(n)=4 for all n≥331,777n \ge 331{,}777n≥331,777.

Both halves of the exact-value statement are kernel-verified. This determines the eventual value of the constant whose existence Choi–Erdős–Szemerédi proved in 1975.

Four values beyond the frozen release

Status: independently checked; release-grade archival pending

After the release freeze, computations produced four more exact values:

h4(n)=4for 65≤n≤68.h_4(n) = 4 \quad \text{for } 65 \le n \le 68.h4​(n)=4for 65≤n≤68.

Each computation returned a solver verdict and an independently checked lower-bound witness. Their release-grade certificates (dual engines + cake_lpr) are not yet archived, so these values do not appear in the paper or the certified Zenodo release.

In plain language

Take the odd numbers in {1,…,2N}\{1,\ldots,2N\}{1,…,2N}. They form half the set, yet contain all six pairwise sums of no four integers: a sum bi+bjb_i + b_jbi​+bj​ is odd only when its terms have opposite parity, and three integers cannot be pairwise opposite in parity. The question is how much more than half you must take before the structure becomes unavoidable.

For positive bib_ibi​ and k=4k = 4k=4, Choi, Erdős, and Szemerédi proved that this excess eventually becomes constant. Fifty years later, the eventual value is known: 4, from n=331,777n = 331{,}777n=331,777 onward. The Lean 4 kernel verifies the proof from the combinatorial recursion through the final arithmetic. SAT computations resolve the reported small cases cell by cell, each with a proof object checked by an independent, formally verified program.

What is not claimed

  • Nothing is claimed about the eventual value of g5g_5g5​. In particular, the small values above do not imply g5(n)=4g_5(n) = 4g5​(n)=4, or even g5(n)≤5g_5(n) \le 5g5​(n)≤5, for all sufficiently large nnn. The paper gives a conditional route and identifies its three remaining gaps.
  • h4(n)=4h_4(n) = 4h4​(n)=4 for 65≤n≤6865 \le n \le 6865≤n≤68 is excluded from the certified release until archival completes.
  • The kernel theorem covers every n≥331,777n \ge 331{,}777n≥331,777 by proof rather than finite enumeration (the problem page notes the general problem “cannot be resolved with a finite computation”).

Verification trail

  • General-nnn theorems: Lean 4 kernel (pinned mathlib toolchain), sorry-free; axiom audits report exactly propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound. The audit logs ship in the release (auditC4P1.log.txt, auditC4Synth.log.txt), and the development byte-pins the upstream formalization it extends.
  • Exact values: every nontrivial cell solved by two independent SAT engines. UNSAT halves carry DRAT/LRAT certificates checked with cake_lpr; SAT halves carry explicit witness sets re-checked by independent code.
  • Internal review included independent re-derivation of the counting lemmas, fresh rebuilds of the full Lean development, and dated checks of the problem’s open status against the literature immediately before release.
